Martijn Motmans

Cloud Engineer | AWS Certified | Python Developer

After several years as a nurse, I made a deliberate switch to cloud engineering. Nursing taught me to stay calm under pressure, take ownership, and never guess when you can verify. Those habits transfer directly to running production infrastructure.

Today I run two production systems on AWS. The first is this site: a Flask application on EC2 behind Nginx, originally provisioned with CloudFormation in a custom VPC, later migrated across regions and re-architected to cut its running cost by 83%. The second is fully serverless: an image-analysis pipeline built on Lambda, Step Functions, Rekognition, and DynamoDB, defined entirely in Terraform and deployed through a GitHub Actions pipeline that stores no AWS credentials.

I hold three AWS certifications, but most of what I know comes from operating these systems for real: debugging IAM policies, tuning costs, setting rate limits and budget circuit breakers, and documenting what broke and why. I'm currently expanding my skills in Docker and containerization.

What makes me different is a background that taught me to manage complexity and take responsibility when it matters. That translates directly into how I build and run cloud systems.
